I don't keep a diary on a regular basis, but when I do, I either use the Notes app on my phone or a Google Doc when I'm on my computer. I recently decided to organize old notes found some fun stuff.

Here's a story from 2017 when I was traveling frequently for work:

As I handed my ID to the TSA agent at the security gate, he said something that sounded like "que sejo".

I repeated what I heard a few times. Que sejo… what does "sejo" mean?

"Ah, Hindi" I nodded with a laborious smile, as I realized he had said "kaise ho" or "what's up" in Hindi. "It sounded like Spanish!"

"Yeah, Hindi! I'm learning!"

He rattled off a few more phrases as I scanned my boarding pass. It was funny, the first thing my mind turned to was Spanish. I never really learned Hindi save for a few months of classes when I was 26 living in India.

He kept shouting random Hindi words as I started to walk towards the bag screening area. I told him that I was born in New Jersey and never learned Hindi, all masked with an exaggerated laugh and smile. I'm not sure it really registered with him.

Did he realize that I probably grew up more similarly to his kids than my parents? That English was my first language and Spanish my second, and really, if I had properly learned one Indian language it would have been Tamil before Hindi?

The exchange was all in good fun; a search by the officer for some lighthearted human connection in an otherwise dull, tension-inducing job. But just imagine how bizarre it would be if I had randomly yelled Polish  phrases to him as I walked into the terminal...

That same day, as I boarded my flight I looked ahead and saw a sleepy-eyed young-but-older-than-me Indian guy boarding immediately ahead of me. He was wearing a flat brim SnapBack; he could have been me.

And he was— it was Kumar, Kal Pen (or Kalpen Modi as the ticket scanner read as I bent over to scan my own ticket)— my doppelgänger and namesake throughout high school when Harold And Kumar hit the big screen.

I began imagining how I would take my headphones out to politely engage him in conversation:

"I met you at Penn when you were teaching a course. We fliered together for Obama 08!"

Or, "I can't tell you how helpful Kumar was to my popularity in high school."

And then, a red flash and a buzzer. Invalid flight. 

"Sir are you going to Portland, ME?"

"Ah, no, Detroit" I offered as I jolted back into reality. The agent was annoyed- another guy with his headphones in wasting time.

I walked to from A26 to A26A, and headed to Detroit.
