ॐ भूर्भुवस्व:
तत्सवितुर्वरेण्यम् ।
भर्गो देवस्य धीमहि ।
धियो यो न: प्रचोदयात् ॥

I moved to Houston in 1997 with my parents and brother. My maternal grandparents, who lived near us in New Jersey, followed months later. When they moved into their new home, we held a small ceremony to bless the house and mark the beginning of a new phase of our collective lives together. 

Our family was not religious. We didn't regularly pray or go to temple or practice superstitions that seem to be the hallmark of "religious" families. My grandma would often say "God is an adjective, not a noun," to be found in the everyday actions and mere existence of all living beings. 

Prayer in order to seek was, therefore, unnecessary. Futile, even. There were no nouns - no Supreme Beings - to appeal to for help. However, prayer for the sake of appreciation could be different; an opportunity to pause and contemplate the beauty of life with all of its complexities. 

So, we moved, we paused, we marked the occasion, and we smelled the roses. Well, jasmine and marigold for this particular Puja. 

— 

I left my job at the [Solana Foundation](https://solana.com/solutions/artists-creators) 2.5 months ago to embark on a new journey. My intention for the first 6 months of this sabbatical from traditional "work" is to focus on resetting my habits around physical health and creative output. So far I've been doing so privately. Now, I'd like to push myself to share more of my activity openly with this as my base. 

With that intention, I'm blessing this new home of mine. I look forward to filling it with the artifacts of experiments and observations and feelings and thoughts. It'll have objects that span media forms and topics, with the belief that a "full stack" approach to understanding the world around me is the only way I can make sense of it all.

When you come inside, I hope those objects will give you a sense of who I am — at this moment in my life, at least. And even if there are no visitors, I hope that I'll have somewhere to take these objects out of my imagination and into mediated space.

May the Earth, Sky, Heaven, and light from the brilliant Sun illuminate our minds.
